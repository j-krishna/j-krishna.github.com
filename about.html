I am a very curious person, intrigued by many things. My thoughts revolve around data visualization, urban planning and transportation, economics, and morality. Since coming to Portland, Oregon for college I have called it my home. The city is a beautiful mix of people, interests, and environment.

Work

I work at Periscopic, a socially-conscious data visualization company. After joining Periscopic as an intern, they took a liking to me. Now I manage many of the great projects we create.

Education

In 2009, I graduated with a bachelors from Reed College. While at Reed I studied mathematics and economics. In my thesis I wrote about a the macroeconomic effects of creative destruction. I am still very interested in the idea of creative destruction and its implications today.

Fun Facts

I get places on my bike. It is a stable steed built by Kona. Like me, it is simple yet efficient; its sole fixed gear gets the job done. Every day I enjoy my ride to work, rain or shine. But let's face it - the ride is especially nice when it is sunny.

Born and raised near Denver, Colorado, I always am attracted towards the outdoors and mountains.

That moment when you understand how or why something works brings me more satisfaction than anything. I also like to make cookies - the chewy, warm, delicious chocolate chip kind.
